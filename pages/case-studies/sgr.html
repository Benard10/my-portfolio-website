Case Study

Kenyan SGR GIS Data Foundation

National infrastructure delivery

Context

The Kenyan Standard Gauge Railway project required reliable geospatial datasets for route alignment, engineering planning, and environmentally responsible implementation across a large corridor.

My Role

I contributed to the creation and validation of corridor GIS datasets, including terrain and ecosystem mapping outputs used by planning and engineering teams.

Constraints

Key constraints included strict precision requirements, cross-functional coordination, and balancing infrastructure needs with ecosystem protection objectives.

Approach

The implementation included detailed dataset development and analysis support:

  • Mapped infrastructure and natural features along the railway corridor.
  • Developed terrain models to support engineering design and construction planning.
  • Produced ecosystem mapping layers to identify environmentally sensitive zones.
  • Compiled and standardized outputs in ArcGIS and AutoCAD for team-wide use.

Impact Metrics

  • Improved confidence in route-alignment planning.
  • Strengthened ecosystem risk visibility for planners.
  • Increased data consistency across planning and implementation teams.
  • Supported more efficient engineering decision cycles.

Skills and Tools

Terrain modeling, ecosystem mapping, geospatial compilation, and infrastructure GIS support.
